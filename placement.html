some MULCH PLACEMENT TIPS

These are some guidelines to keep in mind when applying mulch.

A good standard for mulch depth is 4 inches (100 mm) - this is enough to produce a good insulating cover and keep soil from drying out.

Use a thinner cover ( 2 - 3 inches) on areas with soil that stays quite moist.

Make sure your site drains well - an area that holds water will not benefit from mulch. Covering an excessively moist area with mulch will exasperate the problem as the mulch will keep the moisture from evaporating away.

Before topping up beds with new mulch, rake the old mulch to an even cover and place the new mulch on top.

Mulch around trees and shrubs should extend at least to the drip line in order to cover as much of the root area as possible - the further the mulch cover extends out from the base of the plant, the better.

Avoid placing mulch against the stems of plants and do not pile mulch against the trunks of trees or shrubs. Keep the mulch 2 to 3 inches away from tree trunks to avoid moisture damage to the bark.

Mulch can be applied from spring to snowfall - but keep in mind that as an insulator, a mulch cover will delay frost from coming out of the soil and could delay growing times if applied very early in the season.
